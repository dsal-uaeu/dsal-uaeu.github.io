ETHICS OF THE USE OF AI IN MEDICINE


The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) into the medical sector has raised myriad ethics questions: who does the intellectual property if an AI machine proposes a new drug? Shall a practitioner reveal to the patients the potential diseases they are expected to develop based on AI predictions? etc. Along with the description of numerous applications of AI in medicine, ethical considerations and future perspectives of the integration of AI in the health sector will be discussed in this talk.

Speaker: Dr. Alya Arabi
Biochemistry and Molecular Biology
College of Medicine and Health Sciences

Dr. Alya A. Arabi is an Associate Professor in the Department of Biochemistry, College of Medicine and Health Sciences at the UAEU. She is Honorary Lecturer in the Department of Chemistry at University College London, UK (2017- ). In 2012, Dr. Arabi received her PhD in Chemistry from Dalhousie University, Canada. In 2008, she was awarded a Bachelor of Sciences Honors degree with highest aggregate from Mount Saint Vincent University joint with Dalhousie University, Canada. Her research is focused on biochemical systems using computational tools. She studies 1) the effect of external factors such as electric fields and intercalators on DNA mutations and cancer, and 2) bioisosterism in drug design.
